CRITICAL DISTINCTION: ANALYSIS vs MOTIVATION

**Analysis (Thinking):**
- This is the place to use all available information from the original run and frame it as intuition and thinking process.
- You can directly refer to the CURRENT TRAJECTORY (distilled) information but not to the information from the original trajectory.
- Shows the reasoning process
- Internal monologue, observations leading to hypothesis
- Demonstrates expert thinking patterns
- Can be more exploratory and show uncertainty

**Motivation (Conclusion + Context):**
- States the decision and justification
- "We need to do X because Y. This will accomplish Z."
- Preserves specific details for future reference
- Comprehensive and definitive
- Accumulates knowledge that will appear in future trajectory steps

Think of it as: Analysis is the scratchpad; Motivation is the lab notebook entry.

ANALYSIS CHAIN-OF-THOUGHT GUIDELINES

The analysis shows the THINKING IN PROGRESS, not a finished explanation. Think like an expert muttering to themselves while debugging.

Good patterns:
"I see [observation]... that's interesting because [inference]..."
"Wait, [symptom] combined with [symptom] usually means [hypothesis]..."
"The error says [X], but the real issue is probably [Y] since [reasoning]..."
"Let me think... [A] happens before [B], so if [A] is wrong, then [B] would fail exactly like this..."
"This pattern - [description] - I've seen this when [cause]..."
"Hmm, [observation] suggests [hypothesis], but I should verify [assumption] first..."

Bad patterns:
"Step 34 shows that..." or "The optimized trajectory indicates..." (Only as long as quoted facts are from the previous steps in the optimized trajectory, not from the original trajectory which is supposed only to provide intuitive insights and won't be visible in the final training data)
"We should do X because Y" (that's the motivation, not the thinking)
"The next step is..." (that's also the motivation)
Post-hoc explanations that sound like documentation
Generic statements without concrete observations driving them
Repeating details that belong in motivation (save those for the conclusion)

The analysis should feel like watching someone's reasoning unfold in real-time.
