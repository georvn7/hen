DEBUG TRAJECTORY SCORING

The trajectory shows a sequence of test runs and function fixes that ultimately result in a passing test and successful compilation. Between each run_test-fix_function sequence we have information gathering actions that are currently skipped but could be: log_info, function_info, debug_function, data_info, file_info, call_graph, functions_summary, step_info, search_source 

TASK

Analyze the debugging trajectory and categorize each test-fix sequence by its contribution to passing the test.
Sequence definition: Each sequence starts with a run_test step and includes all actions until the next run_test. Information-gathering actions (log_info, function_info, etc.) are part of sequences but don't need individual scoring and aren't listed in the trajectory.

Merged steps note:

fix_function steps that fix the same function are already procedurally merged and denoted in the trajectory, for example:
NOTE: Merge step 224 into step 229 as it fixes the same function 'cg_emit_index_simple_rec'
The last original step in the merged sequence is the anchor step, for example:
NOTE: Anchor’s own step 229
When listing steps in your response don't list steps that are already merged under a given anchor

CATEGORIES

Score each sequence into ONE category:
1. BLOCKERS
Critical fixes that directly resolved test-blocking issues. Without these, the test could not pass.

2. REGRESSIONS
Attempted to fix critical blockers but introduced new bugs. Good intent, flawed execution.

3. CONTRIBUTORS
Helpful changes that supported success but weren't strictly necessary to pass the test (e.g., better error handling, refactoring, edge case fixes).

4. UNNECESSARY
No meaningful impact on test success, or introduced bugs while fixing non-critical issues. Noise or misdirected effort.

RULES

List ONLY fix_function step numbers (not run_test or info-gathering steps)
Don't list steps that are merged in, instead list their anchor steps.
Provide detailed analysis about your decision. In your reasoning, consider groping steps that are related to similar issues.
Skip sequences you're uncertain about in which category to put—don't force categorization
Base scoring on evidence from trajectory (error messages, outcomes), not assumptions
